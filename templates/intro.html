Introduction

The numbers in springboard diving are a part of a coding system that classifies dives according to their flip position, the dive direction, and the number of somersaults and twists. This system allows judges, divers, and audiences to quickly understand the characteristics of a dive being performed.

Here's a breakdown of how to interpret these numbers:

1. Direction

The first digit categorizes dives into groups based on starting and flip directions:

  • 1 - Front
  • 2 - Back
  • 3 - Reverse
  • 4 - Inward
  • 5 - Twister

2. Position

The last digit represents the position of the diver as they flip:

  • A - Straight
  • B - Pike
  • C - Tuck
  • D - Free

3. Rotation

The middle two digits represent the number of half-flips and half-twists in a dive. For non-twisting dives, this number simply indicates the total number of half-flips.
